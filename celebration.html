Drama Workshop

As the culmination of the project's expressive journey, students participated in a drama workshop facilitated by an expert. This interactive platform allowed them to reflect on their earlier activities—drawings and models—and bring their thoughts and experiences to life through performance art.

Activities included storytelling, improvisation, journaling, and role play. These sessions helped children gain confidence in verbal expression, explore identity, and collaborate with peers in creative ways.

Script and Performance

Children co-created a script based on themes of daily life, challenges, and dreams for a better world. The script emerged organically through dialogue and play. Students acted out stories from their communities—both the joyful and the difficult— while imagining future possibilities such as cleaner neighborhoods, more books, and social equality.

School Mela

The final event, a “School Mela,” showcased all student work to the broader community. It featured:

  • Exhibition: Students' drawings, models, and journals displayed in a curated gallery format.
  • Live Performance: A one-hour play performed by the students, highlighting their learnings and imagination.
  • Community Engagement: Parents, teachers, and neighbors attended, fostering pride and dialogue.
